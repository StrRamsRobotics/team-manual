Motors

helpful site for motors

Components of a motor (helpful video):
  • Brushed (hex motors are brushed)

    • Cheaper to construct

    • Easily repairable

    • No controller required

    • Used in FTC as brushless motors require too much energy from the battery

  • Brushless:

    • Effective at high speeds

    • High efficiency and high output power to size ratio

    • Reduced size

    • Better thermal characteristics

      • Brushes cause friction as they are in direct contact with commutator

  • Servo

    • Servos are motors with a closed feedback loop and high gear reduction commonly used for powering arms

Sensors

helpful site for sensors

  • Light sensor

    • Light sensors are used for detecting light

    • There are two main types of light sensor

      • Photoresistor: resistance varies with change in light intensity

      • Photovoltaic cells (Solar cell): this device is usually considered as an energy source but can be converted into a sensor if combined with capacitors and transistors

  • Ultrasonic sensor

    • Ultrasonic sensors generates high frequency sound waves and receives thses waves in order to find out where an obect is

    • These sensors are most commonly used for distance measurements

  • Acceleration sensor

    • This type of sensor is used for measuring acceleration and tilt.

    • When a robot accelerates and generates vibration, it causes the mass to get compressed and produces an electrical charge which is proportional to the force

    • With acceleration sensor, the robot can measure and determine where the robot is and monitor its movement

  • Temperature sensor

    • Temperature sensor is used for measuring the change in temperature

    • In order to measure the change in temperature, the sensor measures the change in voltage difference

Channels

Usually metal beams used to construct the general body of the robot

  • C channels

    • Tapered flanges

  • U channels:

    • Parallel flanges

Brackets

  • Variety of shapes depending on structure

  • Used to reinforce/support structures

  • For example, it may reinforce the connection between two channels

Rails

  • Normally metal beams with a channel within which other components can slide through

  • This is helpful for components that move in one direction

  • X-Rail

    • It has four channels in which components can slide through

Mounts

Variety of shapes depending on component

As the name suggests, the mount attaches a component to the structure

  • Motor mount

Plates

A flat piece of metal with holes in it

Plates are useful in reinforcing the structure
